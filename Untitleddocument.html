           <h2 class="text-3xl font-semibold text-teal-600 mb-6">Program Overview</h2>

           <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow space-y-4 text-stone-700">

               <p class="text-lg">Welcome to the "Cultivating Curiosity" summer learning program! This interactive guide helps you explore a dynamic, integrated curriculum designed for rising 1st and 2nd graders (ages 6-7) in the Atlanta area.</p>

               <p>The program's core philosophy is built on <strong class="text-teal-700">experiential, active learning</strong>. It meticulously weaves together Georgia Standards of Excellence (GSE) objectives across Science, Social Studies, Mathematics, Physical Education, and Language Arts within every planned outing. A key feature is the age-appropriate introduction of advanced concepts like <strong class="text-teal-700">data gathering and visualization</strong>, aiming to foster critical thinking and analytical skills from a young age.</p>

               <p>By transforming Atlanta's rich resources—from the Georgia Aquarium and Stone Mountain Park (leveraging existing memberships) to free gems like the High Museum of Art and local nature centers—into an extended classroom, this program seeks to:</p>

               <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-1 pl-4">

                   <li>Mitigate summer learning loss.</li>

                   <li>Deepen understanding of upcoming grade-level topics.</li>

                   <li>Promote essential physical activity.</li>

                   <li>Cultivate a lifelong passion for learning and exploration.</li>

               </ul>

               <p>This guide is structured to help you understand the program's foundations (GSE Standards), discover ideal learning locations (Venue Guide), see practical application (Weekly Themes), and get helpful advice (Learning Tips). Let's embark on a summer of discovery and growth!</p>

           </div>

           <h2 class="text-3xl font-semibold text-teal-600 mb-6">Georgia Standards of Excellence (GSE) Explorer</h2>

           <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow">

               <p class="mb-6 text-stone-700">This section provides an overview of the key Georgia Standards of Excellence (GSE) for rising 1st and 2nd graders that form the academic backbone of this summer learning program. Understanding these standards helps in appreciating how each activity and outing is designed to be both enriching and academically relevant. The program aims to reinforce and expand upon these objectives in an engaging, hands-on manner.</p>

                   <h3 class="text-xl font-semibold text-teal-700">Science: Exploring the Natural and Physical World</h3>

                   <p>Science education encourages questioning, observation, and investigation. A key theme is early data literacy – obtaining, evaluating, communicating information, and representing data in tables/graphs. Engineering and problem-solving are also integrated through designing solutions.</p>

                   <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4">

                       <div class="border p-4 rounded-md bg-amber-50">

                           <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Key 1st Grade Objectives:</h4>

                           <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-1 text-sm">

                               <li>Observe, question, investigate patterns (weather, light, sound, magnets, plants/animals).</li>

                               <li>Represent data in tables/graphs.</li>

                               <li>Identify plant parts, compare plant/animal needs, design solutions.</li>

                               <li>Understand how light enables visibility and sound is produced by vibrations.</li>

                           </ul>

                       </div>

                       <div class="border p-4 rounded-md bg-sky-50">

                           <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Key 2nd Grade Objectives:</h4>

                           <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-1 text-sm">

                               <li>Understand stars, sun/moon patterns, shadow changes, moon phases.</li>

                               <li>Investigate properties of matter (solids/liquids, changes of state).</li>

                               <li>Explain force (push/pull) on object motion, design devices, analyze data.</li>

                               <li>Explore life cycles of animals/plants, animal roles in pollination/seed dispersal.</li>

                           </ul>

                       </div>

                   </div>

                   <p class="mt-2 text-sm italic"><strong>Program Connection:</strong> Outings to nature centers, Stone Mountain, and the Aquarium provide ample opportunities for direct observation, data collection (e.g., counting species, tracking shadows), and understanding life cycles and physical forces.</p>

               </div>

                   <h3 class="text-xl font-semibold text-teal-700">Social Studies: Discovering History, Geography, and Civics</h3>

                   <p>Social studies introduces historical figures, geographic concepts, civic understanding, and basic economics. There's a focus on connecting local Atlanta sites to broader narratives and making abstract concepts tangible.</p>

                   <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4">

                       <div class="border p-4 rounded-md bg-amber-50">

                           <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Key 1st Grade Objectives:</h4>

                           <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-1 text-sm">

                               <li>Contributions of historical figures (Franklin, Jefferson, Roosevelt, Carver, etc.).</li>

                               <li>Locate city, county, state, nation, continents/oceans on maps/globes.</li>

                               <li>Identify landforms. Use compass rose.</li>

                               <li>Understand goods/services, scarcity, producers/consumers.</li>

                               <li>Positive character traits (fairness, respect, courage).</li>

                           </ul>

                       </div>

                       <div class="border p-4 rounded-md bg-sky-50">

                           <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Key 2nd Grade Objectives:</h4>

                           <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-1 text-sm">

                               <li>Lives/contributions of Georgia historical figures (MLK Jr., Jackie Robinson, Jimmy Carter).</li>

                               <li>Compare Creek/Cherokee cultures to present.</li>

                               <li>Understand government, rules/laws, roles of elected officials.</li>

                               <li>Use letter/number map grids, map keys, explain geography's impact.</li>

                               <li>Allocation of goods/services.</li>

                           </ul>

                       </div>

                   </div>

                    <p class="mt-2 text-sm italic"><strong>Program Connection:</strong> Visits to MLK Jr. National Historical Park, Georgia Capitol Museum, and discussions at various sites about past vs. present, resource use (e.g., entry fees), and local geography bring these concepts to life.</p>

               </div>

                   <h3 class="text-xl font-semibold text-teal-700">Mathematics: Building Number Sense and Data Literacy</h3>

                   <p>Mathematics focuses on problem-solving, reasoning, and modeling with math in real-life contexts. A critical component is data representation and interpretation through picture and bar graphs.</p>

                   <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4">

                       <div class="border p-4 rounded-md bg-amber-50">

                           <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Key 1st Grade Objectives:</h4>

                           <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-1 text-sm">

                               <li>Count to 120, place value (tens/ones), add/subtract within 20.</li>

                               <li>Order objects by length, express length in units.</li>

                               <li>Represent/interpret data.</li>

                               <li>Solve real-life problems. Reason with time and shapes.</li>

                           </ul>

                       </div>

                       <div class="border p-4 rounded-md bg-sky-50">

                           <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Key 2nd Grade Objectives:</h4>

                           <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-1 text-sm">

                               <li>Place value to 1,000 (hundreds/tens/ones), skip-counting.</li>

                               <li>Add/subtract within 100/1000, foundations for multiplication.</li>

                               <li>Draw picture/bar graphs for data sets (up to four categories).</li>

                               <li>Measure lengths with different units, tell time, solve money problems.</li>

                           </ul>

                       </div>

                   </div>

                   <p class="mt-2 text-sm italic"><strong>Program Connection:</strong> Activities like counting animals at the Aquarium, measuring shadows at Stone Mountain, classifying objects at nature centers, and discussing costs/budgets during outings directly feed into creating simple graphs and applying math skills.</p>

               </div>

                   <h3 class="text-xl font-semibold text-teal-700">Physical Education: Movement, Health, and Well-being</h3>

                   <p>PE emphasizes lifetime fitness, physical activity, and skill development. Active outings inherently align with these standards, promoting gross motor skills, spatial awareness, and social interaction.</p>

                    <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4">

                       <div class="border p-4 rounded-md bg-amber-50">

                           <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Key 1st Grade Objectives:</h4>

                           <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-1 text-sm">

                               <li>Health-enhancing fitness, motor skills (throwing, catching, striking).</li>

                               <li>Move in patterns/directions, balance, weight transfer.</li>

                               <li>Awareness of personal space, goal setting.</li>

                               <li>Responsible social behavior, value of physical activity.</li>

                           </ul>

                       </div>

                       <div class="border p-4 rounded-md bg-sky-50">

                           <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Key 2nd Grade Objectives:</h4>

                           <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-1 text-sm">

                               <li>Continued development of fitness, motor skills, movement patterns.</li>

                               <li>Practice balance/weight transfer, personal space, fitness assessments.</li>

                               <li>Exhibit responsible social behavior, recognize value of physical activity.</li>

                           </ul>

                       </div>

                   </div>

                   <p class="mt-2 text-sm italic"><strong>Program Connection:</strong> Hiking at Stone Mountain, exploring museum exhibits, playing at nature center playgrounds, and even mimicking animal movements at the Aquarium all contribute to physical development and social learning.</p>

               </div>

                   <h3 class="text-xl font-semibold text-teal-700">Language Arts: Communicating and Comprehending</h3>

                   <p>Language Arts development is fostered through rich oral experiences, descriptive language, journaling, and storytelling inspired by outings. The progression is from foundational skills to more complex reading, writing, and symbolic language.</p>

                   <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4">

                       <div class="border p-4 rounded-md bg-amber-50">

                           <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Key 1st Grade Objectives:</h4>

                           <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-1 text-sm">

                               <li>Identify/manipulate syllables/phonemes, blend/segment sounds.</li>

                               <li>Distinguish short/long vowels, decode/encode words (high-frequency).</li>

                               <li>Read with fluency, form legible letters/words.</li>

                               <li>Use common/proper/possessive nouns, complete sentences. Make predictions.</li>

                           </ul>

                       </div>

                       <div class="border p-4 rounded-md bg-sky-50">

                           <h4 class="font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Key 2nd Grade Objectives:</h4>

                           <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-1 text-sm">

                               <li>Identify/produce phoneme-grapheme correspondences (consonants/vowels).</li>

                               <li>Decode/encode two-syllable words, apply syllable types.</li>

                               <li>Read with increasing automaticity/prosody.</li>

                               <li>Write in various genres, use symbolic language (courage, freedom), expand sentences.</li>

                           </ul>

                       </div>

                   </div>

                   <p class="mt-2 text-sm italic"><strong>Program Connection:</strong> Encouraging children to describe observations, ask questions, journal about experiences, learn new vocabulary related to outings, and create stories enhances all aspects of language arts.</p>
